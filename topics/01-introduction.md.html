Topic 01-introduction.md

Introduction

Purpose

Building games can be a lot of fun. It can even be profitable. One part of making games which is neither fun nor profitable is wasting your time trying to evaluate a bunch of different gaming engines and frameworks. You first have to look at platform support, then features and performance, but then you also have to weigh in with documentation and community - oh, let's not forget programming language! In short I grew tired of compromising on framework or engine , so I decided to build it myself. This is what Lugaru offers:

  • Portable across PC, OSX, Linux, iOS, Android.
  • Open-source
  • Text/file-centric rather than IDE-centric
  • Allows me to develop & run locally (on PC/Mac/Linux) but still ship on mobile
  • Highly-productive language, but still performant (Lua)
  • Feature-rich so I don't have to reinvent the wheel (Moai)

In short, I wanted a gaming framework that gave me the productivity of a prototyping while being good enough to ship.

This tutorial

The best way to learn how to do something is to get your hands dirty and try it out yourself. This tutorial is going walk you through building a game using Lugaru. I've picked a simple game: pacman. Are you ready? Let's get going...
